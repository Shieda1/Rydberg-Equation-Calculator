What is the rydberg equation?
The rydberg equation is a calculation of the wavelengths of a spectral line in many chemical elements. This equation was presented as a generalization of the Balmer series for all atomic electron transitions of hydrogen.
How to calculate rydberg equation?
To calculate rydberg equation you should use the following formula:
ΔE = -R * ((1/nf^2) - (1/ni^2)) * Z^2
where:
ΔE = difference in energy between final and initial energy levels.
R = Rydberg's constant
nf = final energy level
ni = initial energy level
z = atomic number
Why use rydberg equation?
The rydberg equation is used in order to predict the wavelength of the light resulting from an electron moving between the energy levels of an atom.
